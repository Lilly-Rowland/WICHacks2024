The Gender Gap in Tech

Despite strides made in recent years, women and gender minorities continue to be underrepresented in STEM disciplines. Which raises the question, why? Is it as simple as a lack of interest? The answer is no. Many girls do have an interest in STEM but sadly lose confidence in their abilities as they get older. This can be due to a variety of factors, including societal pressures, lack of self confidence, or the misconception that math is a “boy subject” and girls just aren’t as equipped for such a field. All of these contribute to girls' declining interest in STEM. Even women who make it through the education process and into the workforce are still at risk for abandoning the subject due to similar reasons. But it doesn’t have to be this way. Through community and education and bringing others up, we can bridge the gender gap in STEM. Please explore our website to learn more about the cause and take some time to think about what you can do to make a difference in this field, gender minority or ally. We’re in this together.
