The Data

Many people have their own personal experience with respect to the gender gap in tech, but data collected through studies can show how truly widespread the issue is. According to data collected and published by the National Science Foundation, women in mathematics, statistics, and computer science were awarded 18,959 bachelors degrees in the year 2014. In the same year, men were awarded 58,701 bachelors degrees in the same fields. Between 2013 and 2015, there was an increase in employed undergraduates in these fields of 21,000 women and 172,000 men.

What this means is that men were more likely to be hired. While women represented 24.4% of the bachelors degrees in mathematics and computer science awarded in 2014, they represented only 10.8% of the job growth of undergraduates with math and computer science degrees from 2013 to 2015.
